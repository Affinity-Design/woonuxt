# ProSkaters Place — Live SEO Reality Check

**Observed:** April 7, 2026  
**Method:** Public-page spot checks against live URLs  
**Purpose:** Record what is actually visible on the live site so planning starts from current reality, not stale documentation.

---

## Executive Readout

The live site shows a split picture.

- Brand pages are the clearest example of successful SEO automation already shipped.
- Category pages and article templates still carry mixed-market wording, duplicated FAQ patterns, and low-differentiation copy blocks.
- Article pages now show some author presence, which means older audit language about zero attribution is outdated.
- Several pages still read like scaled AI content rather than high-confidence editorial assets.

---

## Pages Checked

### Homepage `/`

**Observed strengths**

- Strong commercial breadth and visible trust elements.
- FAQ block exists and covers common beginner questions.
- Internal linking to products, articles, brands, and support is extensive.

**Observed issues**

- H1 remains `ProSkaters Place`, which is brand-first and weak for head-term targeting.
- Duplicate FAQ question about skate fit is still visible.
- Article teasers use repetitive curiosity-hook copy and show `No Comments`, which makes the content system feel thin rather than expert.
- The page still mixes broad merchandising with generic FAQ content rather than a sharper search-intent hierarchy.

**Planning implication**

- Do not treat the homepage FAQ cleanup as complete.

### Brand page `/brand/rollerblade/`

**Observed strengths**

- Long-form brand copy is live.
- FAQ content is live.
- Internal links and retailer-positioning language are live.
- This page aligns closely with the documented brand optimization workflow.

**Observed issues**

- It still needs formal post-rollout QA closure: failed-brand completion, Search Console requests, and rich-results validation are not documented as finished.

**Planning implication**

- Brand automation is proven and should be the model for future workstreams.

### Category page `/shop/inline-skates/`

**Observed strengths**

- There is meaningful body copy and a buying guide below the product listings.
- Internal links to brands, products, and related articles are extensive.
- The page is trying to support both commerce and education.

**Observed issues**

- Market wording is mixed and messy: `Inline Skates United States`, `Inline Skates USA`, and copy referencing both USA and Canada coexist.
- The page includes visible low-quality artifacts such as repeated headings and awkward inline formatting.
- The content leans generic and encyclopedia-like rather than being a sharp commercial landing page.
- Article teasers and supporting blocks still inherit the same templated editorial feel as the homepage.

**Planning implication**

- Category upgrade work is justified and urgent, especially on top commercial pages.

### Category page `/shop/roller-skates/`

**Observed strengths**

- There is substantial supporting copy, buying guidance, FAQs, and related links.
- The page clearly targets a major commercial category.

**Observed issues**

- Market language is inconsistent: United States, USA, Canada, and even Toronto-specific content appear in the same template family.
- There is a clearly wrong or stale FAQ entry: `Where can I roller blade in Toronto?`
- The duplicate `Are roller skates better tight or loose?` pattern is still present.
- One visible link is malformed: `http://shop/roller-blades`.
- The page is overstuffed with generic educational blocks instead of a tighter transactional hierarchy.

**Planning implication**

- This page supports the case for both category rewrites and a separate roller-skates hub page.

### Article `/how-much-does-roller-skating-cost/`

**Observed strengths**

- This is a real traffic-driving article and includes strong commercial internal linking.
- The page now shows `PSP Team` with a link to an author archive.

**Observed issues**

- The older audit assumption of no author attribution is no longer current.
- The page still appears to be carrying the burden of ranking for commercial terms that should likely resolve to category or hub pages.
- Content style remains vulnerable to AI-pattern fatigue.

**Planning implication**

- Update planning docs to reflect partial author progress, but still fix intent routing.

### Article `/off-road-rollerblades/`

**Observed strengths**

- Strong topical targeting, consistent keyword usage, visible FAQ block, and clear commercial CTA.
- Author presence is visible through `PSP Team`.

**Observed issues**

- Copy still has recognizable AI-style patterns: repeated “ultimate guide” structure, generic excitement framing, and overly broad explanatory passages.
- The article is long, but not always dense with unique expertise.

**Planning implication**

- Answer-first formatting and editorial tightening matter more than pure word count.

### Article `/what-to-wear-when-rollerskating/`

**Observed strengths**

- Strong internal linking to commercial destinations.
- Multiple supporting sections and FAQ coverage.
- Author presence is visible.

**Observed issues**

- The page is an example of scaled AI-style editorial structure: repetitive section templates, repeated CTAs, and familiar “ultimate tips” phrasing.
- It is more comprehensive than authoritative.
- The quantity of content risks diluting rather than sharpening the answer.

**Planning implication**

- Top traffic articles need editorial compression, stronger direct answers, and more distinctive expertise.

### Author archive `/author/manager/`

**Observed strengths**

- A real author archive exists.
- Articles appear associated with `PSP Team`.

**Observed issues**

- This does not yet amount to a mature E-E-A-T system.
- The presence of one generic author identity is better than none, but it is not the same as expert-led authorship with bios and credentials.

**Planning implication**

- Future docs should describe author attribution as partial, not absent.

---

## Reality-Based Conclusions

1. Brand pages are the strongest finished SEO asset on the site.
2. The highest-risk template family is not brand pages; it is category pages plus scaled article pages.
3. The current site has enough content volume. It needs better intent control, tighter quality, and cleaner market consistency.
4. Future planning should distinguish between `partial progress`, `live and verified`, and `code exists`.

---

## Recommended Immediate Fixes From This Reality Check

1. Remove duplicate FAQ entries from homepage and roller-skates template surfaces.
2. Clean mixed-market and Toronto-specific wording from key commercial category pages.
3. Fix malformed or low-confidence supporting links on commercial templates.
4. Retrofit the top traffic articles to answer-first structures with less generic AI-style framing.
5. Treat author attribution as a refinement project, not a greenfield project.
